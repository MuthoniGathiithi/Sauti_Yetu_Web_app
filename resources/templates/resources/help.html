Immediate Safety

  • Trust your instincts - if you feel unsafe, you probably are
  • Have a safety plan ready with escape routes
  • Keep important documents and emergency money accessible
  • Tell trusted friends or family about your situation
  • Know where the nearest police station is located
  • Call 999 immediately if in danger

Legal Rights & Support

  • You have the right to live free from violence
  • You can get a protection order against your abuser
  • Free legal aid is available through FIDA Kenya
  • You don't need to prove physical injuries to get help
  • Emotional and psychological abuse are also crimes
  • Call FIDA Kenya: 0800 720 501

Medical & Health Support

  • Seek medical attention even if injuries seem minor
  • Document all injuries with photos and medical reports
  • Get tested for STIs and pregnancy if sexually assaulted
  • Mental health counseling is available and important
  • Many hospitals have special units for GBV survivors
  • You have the right to confidential medical care

Emotional & Mental Health

  • What happened to you is not your fault
  • Healing takes time - be patient with yourself
  • Professional counseling can help you recover
  • Support groups connect you with other survivors
  • It's normal to feel confused, angry, or scared
  • You deserve love, respect, and happiness

Safe Housing & Shelter

  • Safe houses are available for women and children
  • You can stay temporarily while planning next steps
  • Shelters provide food, clothing, and basic needs
  • Your location will be kept confidential
  • Children can continue schooling from safe houses
  • Contact local NGOs for shelter information

Financial & Economic Support

  • Emergency financial assistance may be available
  • Job training and placement programs exist
  • Microfinance options for starting small businesses
  • Legal aid to recover property or assets
  • Child support enforcement services
  • Connect with women's economic empowerment groups

Digital Safety Tips

Using This Platform Safely

Always use private browsing mode, clear your browser history after use, and never access from shared computers if possible. Your safety online is just as important as your physical safety.

Phone & Communication Safety

Use a trusted friend's phone to call for help if needed. Be careful about text messages and calls that could be monitored. Consider getting a separate phone for safety communications.

Social Media Precautions

Avoid posting your location or daily routines. Check your privacy settings regularly. Be cautious about who can see your posts and personal information.

Creating a Safety Plan

Plan escape routes from your home and workplace. Keep emergency money and important documents ready. Identify safe places you can go and people you can trust.
